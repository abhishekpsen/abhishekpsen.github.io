Predicting Patient Mortality in ICU

We are facing an explosion in modern Electronic Health Record data, containing an increasingly large amount of important information about the medical conditions of patients. If this data is processed correctly, thousands of lives can be saved by pre-emptive action. However, it is neither straightforward nor scalable for humans (doctors, nurses, medical professionals) to process this ’big’ data and glean insights. In this project, I predict mortality as an acuity surrogate to help doctors identify which patients need immediate care. I experiment with various data from the MIMIC III dataset as features, aiming to find the perfect combination that gives the highest accuracy.

The most interesting part of the project was the finding that information from Clinical Notes almost always improved the AUC score. I used Latent Dirichlet Allocation to model the topics in the Natural Language data from the Clinical Notes. The entire project was primarily implemented in Apache Spark. Python and ScikitLearn were used too. Read the complete report here for the details of our approach and implementation details and check out the presentation
