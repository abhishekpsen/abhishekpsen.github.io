My first Full Time job

I was recruited out of college by Credit Suisse to join the Mumbai office. I joined the Program Trading Team (think large baskets of stocks getting executed at the exchange at once) as a Developer, starting with small bug-fixes at first and handling major feature implementation tasks later. I worked on a web application that allows non Credit Suisse brokers to have their orders executed via our platforms. More executions, more commissions, yayy ! :D My responsibilities were primarily implementing new features and fixing security loopholes. I also implemented major functionalities requested by the business (read Traders) into our flagship Global Portfolio Management and Trading platform.

I got to learn a fair bit of Java, JSP, security, C# and Windows Presentation Foundation development, the Agile methodology and Software Development Life Cycle in the process. Oh yes, and some of this too!

After a year with Program Trading, I moved to the Advanced Executions Services (a la Algorithmic & High Frequency Trading). Our clients in this case were our in-house Algo Traders and salespersons. To ensure that Credit Suisse algorithms were beating the sh*t out of every other algorithm out there in the market every single time, it was necessary to have accurate monitoring, scoring and reporting tools. I was part of a team that maintained, enhanced, consolidated and wrote several new tools for comparing Credit Suisse executions against market conditions. This was also when I really fell in love with Python and I optimized and migrated a large chunk of really ancient batch jobs to Python. I also automated some tasks by writing a few nifty scripts. Another app I wrote here was a job scheduling and monitoring tool, a cron like application, on steroids!

Technologies I used here were mainly C++ and Python and some C#.

Coming from an absolute zero background in financial technologies, I was challenged very often at work but was fortunate enough to find very supportive mentors, managers and colleagues. I loved the feeling of pushing code to production and watch our traders make a killing out there in the markets :P

After a short but great run (2013-2015) with Credit Suisse, I left the firm to join the Masters in CS program at Georgia Tech.
